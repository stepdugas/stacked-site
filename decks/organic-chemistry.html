Organic Chemistry Essentials

Core organic chemistry concepts including functional groups, reactions, and nomenclature.

Alkane
Single bonds only (C-C); saturated hydrocarbon; suffix: -ane; general formula: CnH2n+2; sp3 hybridized
Alkene
Contains C=C double bond; unsaturated; suffix: -ene; general formula: CnH2n; sp2 hybridized; undergoes addition reactions
Alkyne
Contains C≡C triple bond; suffix: -yne; general formula: CnH2n-2; sp hybridized; linear geometry
Alcohol (-OH)
Hydroxyl group; suffix: -ol; hydrogen bonding = higher boiling point; primary, secondary, tertiary based on carbon substitution
Aldehyde (-CHO)
Carbonyl at end of chain; suffix: -al; can be oxidized to carboxylic acid; Tollens test (silver mirror)
Ketone (C=O)
Carbonyl within chain; suffix: -one; cannot be easily oxidized; common solvent (acetone)
Carboxylic Acid (-COOH)
Suffix: -oic acid; acidic (donates H+); forms esters with alcohols; found in vinegar (acetic acid)
Ester (-COOR)
Formed from acid + alcohol (condensation); suffix: -oate; fruity smells; found in fats and oils
Amine (-NH2)
Contains nitrogen; suffix: -amine; basic (accepts H+); found in amino acids and drugs
Ether (-O-)
Oxygen between two carbons; relatively inert; lower boiling point than alcohols; common solvent (diethyl ether)
SN1 Reaction
Substitution, nucleophilic, unimolecular; two steps; carbocation intermediate; favored by tertiary substrates, polar protic solvents; racemization
SN2 Reaction
Substitution, nucleophilic, bimolecular; one step, backside attack; favored by primary substrates, strong nucleophile, polar aprotic solvents; inversion
E1 Elimination
Unimolecular; two steps; carbocation intermediate; favored by tertiary, weak base, heat; Zaitsev product (more substituted alkene)
E2 Elimination
Bimolecular; one step, concerted; favored by strong base, heat, anti-periplanar geometry; Zaitsev product
Markovnikov's Rule
In HX addition to alkenes, H adds to carbon with MORE hydrogens; positive charge on more substituted carbon (more stable)
Anti-Markovnikov
Opposite of Markovnikov; occurs with peroxides (HBr + ROOR); hydroboration-oxidation gives anti-Markovnikov alcohol
Zaitsev's Rule
In elimination reactions, the major product is the more substituted (more stable) alkene
Chirality
A molecule that is non-superimposable on its mirror image; has a chiral center (4 different groups on carbon); R/S configuration
Enantiomers
Mirror-image stereoisomers; same physical properties except optical rotation; opposite R/S configuration
Diastereomers
Stereoisomers that are NOT mirror images; different physical properties; different R/S at some but not all chiral centers
Hybridization
sp3: tetrahedral, 109.5°. sp2: trigonal planar, 120°. sp: linear, 180°. Determines molecular geometry
Electronegativity in Organic
F > O > N > Cl > Br > C > H; determines bond polarity and acidity; inductive effect
Resonance
Delocalization of electrons across adjacent p orbitals; stabilizes molecules; shown with multiple Lewis structures
Carbocation Stability
Tertiary > Secondary > Primary > Methyl; more substituted = more stable; hyperconjugation and inductive effects
Acidity Trends
More stable conjugate base = stronger acid; factors: electronegativity, size, resonance, induction, hybridization
Hydrogen Bonding
Strong intermolecular force; requires H bonded to F, O, or N; increases boiling point; key in DNA, proteins, water
IUPAC Nomenclature Steps
1) Find longest chain 2) Number from end nearest substituent 3) Name substituents 4) Assign numbers 5) Alphabetize
Aromatic Compounds
Benzene ring (C6H6); planar, cyclic, conjugated; 4n+2 pi electrons (Hückel's rule); very stable; undergoes EAS, not addition
Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution
Aromatic ring attacks electrophile; H replaced by E+; types: halogenation, nitration, sulfonation, Friedel-Crafts
Oxidation in Organic
Loss of H or gain of O; primary alcohol → aldehyde → carboxylic acid; secondary alcohol → ketone; use KMnO4, CrO3, PCC
