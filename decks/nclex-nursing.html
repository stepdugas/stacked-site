NCLEX-RN Nursing Essentials

Essential nursing concepts for the NCLEX-RN exam covering patient care, pharmacology, and clinical procedures.

ABCs of Assessment
Airway, Breathing, Circulation — always assess in this priority order
Maslow's in Nursing
Physiological needs first, then Safety, Love/Belonging, Esteem, Self-Actualization — prioritize care accordingly
Vital Signs Normal Ranges
HR: 60-100, BP: <120/80, RR: 12-20, Temp: 97.8-99.1°F, SpO2: 95-100%
Glasgow Coma Scale
Eye (1-4) + Verbal (1-5) + Motor (1-6) = 3-15; <8 = severe brain injury, intubation needed
Informed Consent
Patient must understand procedure, risks, benefits, alternatives; must be competent; nurse witnesses signature, doctor explains
HIPAA
Protects patient health information; only share with those involved in care; violations have legal consequences
Hand Hygiene
Single most important measure to prevent infection; wash 20+ seconds; before/after patient contact; use alcohol-based if no visible soil
Standard Precautions
Treat all blood/body fluids as potentially infectious; gloves, hand hygiene; applies to ALL patients regardless of diagnosis
Contact Precautions
Gown + gloves for MRSA, C. diff, scabies; dedicated equipment; private room preferred
Droplet Precautions
Surgical mask within 3 feet; for flu, pertussis, meningitis; private room; no special air handling
Airborne Precautions
N95 respirator; negative pressure room; for TB, measles, varicella, COVID
Medication Rights
Right patient, Right drug, Right dose, Right route, Right time, Right documentation, Right reason, Right response
Insulin Types
Rapid (lispro, 15min onset), Short (regular, 30min), Intermediate (NPH, 2-4hr), Long (glargine, no peak, 24hr)
Digoxin
Cardiac glycoside; check apical HR for 1 full minute before giving; hold if HR < 60; monitor potassium (low K = toxicity)
Warfarin (Coumadin)
Anticoagulant; monitor PT/INR (therapeutic INR: 2-3); antidote: Vitamin K; avoid leafy greens (vitamin K foods)
Heparin
Anticoagulant; monitor aPTT (therapeutic: 1.5-2x control); antidote: protamine sulfate; SubQ or IV
ACE Inhibitors (-pril)
Lisinopril, enalapril; for HTN/heart failure; monitor for cough, hyperkalemia, angioedema; avoid in pregnancy
Beta Blockers (-olol)
Metoprolol, atenolol; slow HR, lower BP; hold if HR < 60 or BP < 90/60; don't stop abruptly
Seizure Precautions
Padded side rails, suction at bedside; during seizure: protect head, don't restrain, time it, turn on side after
Fall Risk Assessment
Morse Fall Scale; interventions: bed alarm, non-skid socks, call light within reach, toileting schedule
Chest Tube Care
Keep drainage system below chest level; monitor for air leak (bubbling); never clamp; assess drainage color/amount
NG Tube Care
Confirm placement by X-ray before first use; check residual before feeding; elevate HOB 30-45°; flush with water
Foley Catheter Care
Secure tubing; keep bag below bladder; peri-care every shift; monitor output; remove ASAP to prevent CAUTI
Blood Transfusion
Verify with 2 nurses; use Y-tubing with NS only; first 15 min = highest risk; monitor for fever, hives, dyspnea
Transfusion Reaction
Stop transfusion immediately; keep IV open with NS; notify MD; monitor vitals; send blood bag to lab
Hyperglycemia Signs
Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, fruity breath (DKA), Kussmaul respirations, dehydration
Hypoglycemia Signs
Shakiness, sweating, tachycardia, confusion, irritability; treat with 15g fast-acting carbs, recheck in 15 min
Heart Failure Left-Sided
Pulmonary symptoms: dyspnea, orthopnea, crackles, cough, fatigue; backward failure into lungs
Heart Failure Right-Sided
Systemic symptoms: peripheral edema, JVD, hepatomegaly, weight gain; backward failure into body
MI Symptoms
Chest pain (crushing, radiating to arm/jaw), diaphoresis, nausea, dyspnea; women may have atypical symptoms
Stroke Assessment
FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911; ischemic vs hemorrhagic
DVT Prevention
SCDs, early ambulation, anticoagulants; assess for calf pain, warmth, swelling; elevate extremity
Wound Care Basics
Clean to dirty; moist wound healing; assess COCA: Color, Odor, Consistency, Amount of drainage
Pain Assessment
5th vital sign; use numeric (0-10), FACES, or FLACC scale; reassess after intervention
Patient Education
Teach-back method; assess readiness to learn; use simple language; document what was taught
Delegation (RN)
RN cannot delegate: assessment, teaching, evaluation, care planning; CAN delegate: vital signs, ADLs, I&O to UAP
Electrolyte: Potassium (K+)
Normal: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L; low = muscle weakness, dysrhythmias; high = peaked T waves, cardiac arrest; never push IV
Electrolyte: Sodium (Na+)
Normal: 135-145 mEq/L; low = confusion, seizures; high = thirst, dry mucous membranes; monitor fluid balance
Electrolyte: Calcium (Ca2+)
Normal: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL; low = Trousseau/Chvostek sign, tetany; high = kidney stones, bone pain
Acid-Base: Metabolic Acidosis
pH < 7.35, HCO3 < 22; causes: DKA, renal failure, diarrhea; Kussmaul respirations; treat underlying cause
Acid-Base: Respiratory Alkalosis
pH > 7.45, pCO2 < 35; cause: hyperventilation; have patient breathe into paper bag; address anxiety
Pre-Op Nursing Care
NPO status, consent signed, remove jewelry/dentures, mark surgical site, baseline vitals, anxiety management
Post-Op Nursing Care
Monitor vitals q15min, assess surgical site, pain management, I&O, encourage deep breathing/incentive spirometry
Pressure Injury Prevention
Reposition q2h; keep skin dry; adequate nutrition; Braden Scale assessment; pressure-relieving devices
Isolation Gowning Order
Put on: hand hygiene → gown → mask → goggles → gloves. Remove: gloves → goggles → gown → mask → hand hygiene
SBAR Communication
Situation (what's happening), Background (context), Assessment (your clinical judgment), Recommendation (what you need)
Code Blue Response
Call for help, begin CPR (30:2), attach AED/defibrillator, establish IV access, administer epinephrine q3-5min
Pediatric Considerations
Calculate meds by weight (mg/kg); smaller drug doses; assess fontanels in infants; use FLACC for pain
Maternal: Fetal Heart Rate
Normal FHR: 110-160 bpm; accelerations = reassuring; late decelerations = uteroplacental insufficiency (turn mom on left side, O2, stop Pitocin)
End-of-Life Care
Comfort measures; manage pain; family support; advance directives; hospice referral; pronouncement of death
