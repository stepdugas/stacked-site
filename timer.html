Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo. It breaks work into 25-minute focused intervals (called "pomodoros") separated by 5-minute breaks. After four pomodoros, you take a longer 15-30 minute break. It helps maintain focus and prevent burnout during study sessions.

Why is the Pomodoro Technique effective for studying?

Research shows that short, focused study sessions with breaks are more effective than marathon cramming. The Pomodoro Technique works with your brain's natural attention cycles — combating mental fatigue, improving concentration, and helping you retain more information over time.

How many Pomodoro sessions should I do per day?

Most productivity experts recommend 8-12 pomodoros (4-6 hours of focused work) per day. Students studying for exams often find 6-8 pomodoros ideal. The key is consistency — four focused pomodoros beats eight distracted hours every time.
